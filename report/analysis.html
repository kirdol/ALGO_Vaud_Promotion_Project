Analysis

Distribution of the days spent per month

In order to show the possible seasonality of visits, we decided to look at the number of days spent per month of the year in the canton of Vaud.

We can clearly see two major moments in the year when there is an increase in the number of days spent in Vaud: March and July. This could be influenced by national holidays.

Nightstay Category Distribution per Continent

Let’s now look at the percentage of nightstay trips per category and continent. In the survey, visitors were asked to select the type of trip they took from four options, as explained in the legend of the graph. So here is the graph of the distribution of the type of trip for each continent.

In general, it can be seen that most visitors from continents far away from Switzerland come to Switzerland for one or more nights during their trip in Europe. In addition, visitors living closer to Switzerland, such as Europeans, mostly stay for one night in Switzerland. This is probably due to the large number of visitors surveyed who come from Switzerland but from other cantons or from neighboring countries such as Germany and France, all of which together account for around 50% of the visitors surveyed. We can also see that few people making a European tour would spend only one night in Switzerland.

Global Frenquency of Nightstays in Switzerland

Here is the distribution of the frequencies of the number of nights spent during a trip in Switzerland.

As the question asked of visitors was the number of nights spent in Switzerland, it is difficult to know how many nights were spent in the canton of Vaud. We have therefore decided to use the total number of overnight stays in the country in our analysis. In addition, due to the structure of the survey, most of our data contained NAs (according to the answer to question F16, some people could not answer question F20, so their answer was reported as an NA). We have therefore decided to count the NAs as 1 overnight stay, as it is not possible to know if they only spent 1 day in Vaud without staying overnight.

We can therefore see that the majority of visitors generally spend only one night in the canton, with few people staying for more than a week.

Distribution Percentage Nightstays in Switzerland over Entire Trip

Here is the distribution of the number of nights spent in Switzerland over the whole trip.

As this graph shows, we notice that over visitor trips to Europe, most people will come to Switzerland for 20% of their trip, and few will spend more than 75% of their trip in the country.

Distribution of Arrivals per Nationality

Now let’s explore the distribution of the arrivals by nationality over the year. The full table of the arrivals can be found in the ?@sec-data-arrivals.

On this graph, it can be seen that the size of the dots representing the monthly number of arrivals of a nationality, scaled over the total number of arrivals of that particular nationality over the year, varies considerably according to the nationality. Unfortunately, due to the small number of data collected, some countries are represented only once in the whole year. In addition, arrivals vary according to country of origin: most countries have the same main arrival period in March and June, as seen before, but this is not the case for every country, showing differences in seasonality.

Average spending by travel group type

Here we have calculated the average total CHF spent during a trip to Vaud per person, depending on who the respondent has traveled with. All the bars in this plot are non-mutually exclusive responses, meaning respondents could select multiple options, except for the bar “Only with partner”, in which case the respondent only selected “traveled with partner”.

Travel group type by nation

Here we can see the group types of travelers (Alone, with partner, with children, etc.) For each of the top 5 nations of origin of tourists.

  • Swiss
  • French
  • British
  • German
  • American

Swiss tourists are more likely than other nations to travel with children. They are also most likely to be traveling with a partner and pets. This suggests that they are more likely to be visiting as a family than other tourists.

German tourists are the most likely of these countries to be traveling only with their partner, as a couple.

American tourists have by far the highest percentage of tourists traveling alone as well as with a group of unknown people. This suggests they are more likely to be on a solo trip to Switzerland and also be on an organized group tour with other strangers.

Spending by District

It is interesting for us to combine the expense variables with the district ones in order to see in which category of spending the travelers spend the most money for each district.

In all cases, overnight stays are the category most spent on. It is followed mainly by food and drinks, transport, and souvenirs. Looking at the Ouest Lausannois district, we notice that travelers also spend on other expenses.
